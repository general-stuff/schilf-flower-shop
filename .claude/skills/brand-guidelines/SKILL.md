# Flower Shop Brand Styling

## Overview

Use this skill to apply a refined flower-shop visual identity to content and artifacts. The style should feel like a premium florist: elegant, welcoming, colorful, fresh, and artistic, inspired by hand-crafted bouquets and seasonal blooms.

## Brand Direction

The design language should feel:

- Elegant, not flashy
- Colorful, but balanced and tasteful
- Romantic and natural
- Premium boutique rather than rustic farmhouse
- Fresh, airy, and artistic

Avoid harsh contrast, neon tones, overly corporate layouts, or anything that feels tech-branded or minimal to the point of coldness.

## Brand Guidelines

### Color Palette

Use a bouquet-inspired palette with soft neutrals, petal tones, and botanical accents.

**Core Colors:**

- Ivory: `#faf7f2` - Main light background
- Charcoal Brown: `#3d342f` - Primary text and dark accents (contrast ~13:1 on ivory)
- Deep Taupe: `#6e5f57` - Secondary text and neutral elements (contrast ~5:1 on ivory)
- Blush Cream: `#f3e7df` - Subtle backgrounds and panels

**Floral Accent Colors:**

- Deep Rose: `#a84060` - Romantic highlight (contrast ~6.5:1 on ivory)
- Terracotta Coral: `#b85530` - Warm accent (contrast ~5.5:1 on ivory)
- Plum Lavender: `#7050a0` - Elegant secondary accent (contrast ~6:1 on ivory)
- Forest Sage: `#4a6e45` - Botanical accent (contrast ~6:1 on ivory)
- Deep Leaf Green: `#3d6040` - Strong natural contrast (contrast ~7:1 on ivory)
- Amber Gold: `#8a6c10` - Warm cheerful highlight (contrast ~5.5:1 on ivory)

**Decorative-Only Colors (backgrounds, borders, shapes — not for text):**

- Soft Blush: `#e8b8c4` - Light petal tone for decorative use
- Peach Tint: `#f0c8a8` - Warm petal background
- Lavender Mist: `#d4c0e8` - Soft accent for shapes and fills
- Sage Mist: `#b8d0b4` - Light botanical tint
- Butter Cream: `#f0e0a0` - Soft decorative highlight

### Color Usage Rules

- Use ivory or blush cream as the default background base.
- Use charcoal brown for primary text instead of pure black.
- Use deep taupe for secondary text, captions, and metadata.
- Use floral accent colors (deep rose, terracotta, plum lavender, etc.) sparingly and intentionally for interactive elements, headings, badges, or emphasis.
- Reserve decorative-only colors exclusively for backgrounds, borders, fills, or shapes — never as text color.
- Prefer forest sage or deep leaf green for dividers, badges, or subtle emphasis.
- Keep layouts airy; do not oversaturate every element with color.
- When multiple accents are used together, keep one dominant and the others supporting.

## Typography

### Font Pairing

- **Headings**: Playfair Display (Google Font) with Georgia fallback
- **Body Text**: Lora (Google Font) with Georgia fallback
- **Optional Accent Font**: Poppins (Google Font) with Arial fallback for small labels, prices, tags, or modern UI-like elements

### Typography Style

- Headings should feel editorial and luxurious.
- Body text should feel warm, readable, and graceful.
- Use sentence case more often than all caps.
- Avoid overly bold heavy typography.
- Use generous spacing and elegant text hierarchy.

### Font Application

- Large headings: Playfair Display
- Subheadings: Playfair Display or Poppins depending on layout
- Body copy and descriptions: Lora
- Small labels, buttons, price callouts, or metadata: Poppins

## Visual Style Rules

### Layout

- Favor clean layouts with soft spacing and breathing room.
- Rounded corners are welcome when subtle and refined.
- Prefer asymmetry inspired by bouquet composition over rigid symmetry.
- Keep the overall composition graceful and premium.

### Decorative Treatment

- Use floral-inspired accent colors in shapes, separators, borders, or highlights.
- Thin rules, soft fills, and light layered panels work well.
- Decorative elements should feel petal-like, organic, and light.
- Avoid clutter, heavy shadows, or loud graphic effects.

### Emphasis

- Use italic styling sparingly for romantic or editorial flavor.
- Prefer color emphasis over bold-heavy emphasis.
- Use accent colors to draw attention to promotions, featured arrangements, or seasonal notes.

## Shapes and Graphic Elements

- Use soft organic shapes where appropriate.
- Thin borders and muted color blocks are preferred over thick outlines.
- Accent shapes may rotate through rose pink, coral, lavender, and sage.
- Botanical green should ground compositions when multiple floral tones are present.
- Avoid sharp geometric dominance unless balanced by soft colors and spacing.
